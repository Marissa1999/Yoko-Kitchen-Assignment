Mizutaki: Chicken Stew

4 Servings & Ready in 45 Minutes


Main Ingredients:

  • 800 g chicken thighs
  • 1/4 Chinese cabbage
  • 1 Japanese long onion (naganegi)
  • 4 shiitake mushrooms
  • chrysanthemum greens (shungiku)
  • 4 to 8 rolled komaki yuba skin
  • shredded daikon radish
  • powdered chili
  • 2 bunches spring onions

    • Chicken Broth:

  • 2 chicken bones
  • 1 piece of kombu, 10 cm square
  • 50 g short-grain rice
  • 3 L water

    • Ponzu Sauce:

  • 100 ml dashi stock
  • 50 ml vinegar
  • 50 ml soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp mirin
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice

    • Instructions:

    1. Briefly boil the 2 chicken bones, then transfer to cold water. Rinse off any blood and other undesired matter, then dry.

    2. Place the washed chicken carcasses, kombu, rice, and 3 L of water in a pot and heat. When it comes to a boil, skim off any foam and reduce heat to medium-low to simmer for about an hour.

    3. Use a potato masher to crush the chicken bones' bones. Once the broth becomes white and opaque, filter it through a strainer, then once more through cheesecloth.

    4. Briefly boil the chicken thighs. Rinse off any blood and other undesired matter, then blot off with a paper towel.

    5. Pour the filtered broth in a pot and heat. Add the washed chicken thighs and heat until fully cooked through. Skim off any foam that forms on top and remove the chicken thighs.

    6. Briefly boil the Chinese cabbage, then strain. Roll up using a rolling mat, then cut into easy-to-eat pieces.

    7. Cut the naganegi long onion on the bias, and make decorative cuts into the caps of the shiitake mushrooms. Tear the tender parts of the shungiku.

    8. Chop the spring onions finely, then wash it briefly and strain.

    9. Arrange the vegetables and other ingredients on a dish. Put the chicken broth and chicken thighs in a donabe clay pot and heat.

    10. Place the remaining chicken thighs in a dish and add a little bit of the chicken broth.

    11. Put the condiments (spring onions and ichimi oroshi) and ponzu sauce in dishes.

    12. At the table, add a little bit of salt and pepper to small cups and add just the chicken broth, to enjoy the flavor of the broth on its own.

    13. Next, put the chicken thighs in a separate bowl and try with the condiments and ponzu. Put the vegetables into the donabe clay pot and heat until cooked through before eating.

    Itadakimasu! / Enjoy!
