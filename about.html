About How It All Started...

And It's All Thanks to John Lennon

Yoko's Kitchen came about during the early 1970s in London. One day, a young Japanese woman named Yoko Ono was cooking for the first time in her own house kitchen. Finding the freshest ingredients possible, she decided to concoct her favorite oriental dish: kimchi. As the smell of her cooking filled the entire house, her husband John Lennon came home from a joyful day at the music studio. He inhaled deeply, breathing in the pleasant fumes of a recipe. With full of peace and love, he came to her and told her, that she'll become a great chef one day. She blushed and thanked him for his kind compliment. As John sat down at the table, he began to read a newspaper about the troubled war incidents in Vietnam. He felt bad, and since he was a peace activist, he wanted to do something about it. Once night came, John was talking to Yoko, about her starting a Japanese restaurant. She agreed and went to sleep. Silently, John went to the telephone and called some of her relatives from Japan. Once he completed his explanation, they all agreed. The next day, Yoko woke up and went to the kitchen. John was already there, preparing a simple English breakfast. He discussed the good news to her, telling that her relatives are planning to open a new restaurant for her. He also told her about the poor people in Vietnam, which he willingly wants to raise money for charity and peace. Yoko was very delighted and pleased, deciding to call the restaurant Yoko's Kitchen. And from that day forth, Yoko's Kitchen has been lasting for at least 50 years, where not only restaurant food is served, but cooking classes and catering are offered. Yoko Ono managed to hire talented Japanese chefs, who are fantastic at mastering many Japanese dishes. It gives the chance to enhance the compassion of many individuals that not only enjoy cooking, but raise awareness for the poor.
